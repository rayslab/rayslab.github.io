**Swarnima Singh**

*Graduate Student*

I hold a Master’s degree in Biotechnology from Patna University. In my previous work at CSIR-CDRI, I standardized protocols for genetic transformation in plant models using Agrobacterium and gained proficiency in molecular and plant tissue culture techniques. I have always been passionate about neural development and the bidirectional relationship between sophisticated brain functions and behaviour; this passion motivated me to pursue a PhD in neuroscience.

I qualified for CSIR JRF in June 2024 and joined CHINTA in August 2025. During my time at CHINTA, I became familiar with various aspects of neuroscience, including sensory morphogenesis, electrophysiology, and cortical organoid culture for understanding neurodegenerative disorders. I also gained introductory experience in Python programming and computational modelling. I am now part of the Navigation and Neurostimulation Lab as a graduate student under Dr. Subhasis Ray, where I hope to explore how the insect brain processes complex sensory information and forms associations between them.

Outside the laboratory, I enjoy traveling, reading, art, listening to good music, and spending quality time with loved ones. In the future, I aspire to establish my own laboratory and champion women in STEM to foster greater inclusion and innovation in science.
