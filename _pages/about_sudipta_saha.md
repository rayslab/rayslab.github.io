**Sudipta Saha**

*Graduate Student*

I hold a B.Sc. Honours in Zoology from Siliguri College, followed by a Masters in Biotechnology from The Oxford College of Science. During my masters I worked in microbiology, characterizing pectinase producing bacteria from soil sample. In this time, I started exploring neuroscience literature which I got introduced to while preparing for CSIR NET examination. I then joined CSIR-IICT where I worked on checking the therapeutic efficacy of mental nanoparticles against gliobastoma and other cancerous outgrowths.

I was awarded the CSIR JRF in December 2024 and subsequently joined as a PhD student at CHINTA, TCG CREST in August 2025. After short rotation stints, I am now a member of Navigation and Neurosimulation Lab. I am fascinated by how the tiny insect brains process the wide modalities of sensory inputs, like olfaction and audition, and make sense of the world around them.

Beyond the academic pursuits, I like cooking, reading books and playing guitar. I consider myself a generalist with a wide range of interests, be it the genre of book to read, or the kind of meal to cook.
